\begin{abstract}
We study cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) transfer to Danish using multilingual transformer encoders (mBERT and XLM-R), comparing English and Norwegian as source languages across three random seeds. Our main finding is that Norwegian is the better zero-shot transfer source for Danish on the held-out test set: +1.86 F1 for XLM-R ($p=0.10$) and +3.36 F1 for mBERT ($p=0.046$, significant at $\alpha=0.05$). Single-seed development results were misleading --- seed 42 happened to favour Norwegian XLM-R, and averaged across three seeds English XLM-R is marginally ahead on dev --- which underscores the importance of multi-seed evaluation. The Norwegian advantage is consistent with Norwegian being 5.7$\times$ closer to Danish than English in WALS syntactic feature space (cosine distance 0.021 vs 0.119). When fine-tuning on Danish data, an English mBERT model achieves the overall best transfer result (89.4 dev strict F1), showing that source-language data volume can outweigh typological distance once target-language supervision is available. We also report unlabeled and loose span F1, per-entity-type results, a data-efficiency curve, and identify one prediction file as a misaligned artifact.
\end{abstract}
