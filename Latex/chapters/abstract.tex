\begin{abstract}
We study cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) transfer to Danish using multilingual transformer models. The report follows the project proposal and expands it into a full ACL-style paper with detailed experiments, linguistic distance analysis, and error inspection. Our main finding is that Norwegian is the better source language for Danish transfer: it gives higher zero-shot performance than English, and the advantage is consistent with typological and genealogical closeness. XLM-R is the strongest encoder overall, while Danish-only supervision remains the most reliable option once enough labeled data is available. We also identify one English-to-Danish zero-shot prediction file as a stale or mismatched artifact rather than a real result.
\end{abstract}
